# Milestone 2: Image Segmentation

This folder focuses on separating the plant leaf from its background, ensuring that the AI model learns to recognize disease patterns on the leaf itself rather than memorizing irrelevant background details (like soil, hands, or shadows).

## Overview

The `ImageSegmentation.ipynb` notebook implements the segmentation pipeline. It isolates the region of interest (the leaf) from the rest of the image.

This pipeline leverages:
- **OpenCV** (`cv2`) for traditional computer vision and image processing.
- **Mask R-CNN** (Region-based Convolutional Neural Networks) implementation principles for advanced instance segmentation.
- **NumPy** for matrix operations.
- **Matplotlib** for visualization.

## Segmentation Techniques Applied

The following steps are used to achieve accurate segmentation:

### 1. Color Space Conversion & Thresholding
- Images are converted from RGB to HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) or LAB color spaces. These color spaces separate color information from brightness, making it easier to isolate the green hues of the leaves regardless of lighting conditions.
- **Otsu's Binarization:** An automatic thresholding method used to quickly create a rough binary mask separating the foreground (leaf) from the background.

### 2. GrabCut Algorithm
- **OpenCV's GrabCut** is the core algorithm used here. It is an iterative segmentation method that uses Graph Cuts.
- It takes a bounding box (or a rough mask from Otsu's) as input.
- It models the foreground and background using Gaussian Mixture Models (GMMs).
- Through iterative energy minimization, it refines the boundary between the leaf and the background, providing a highly accurate mask.

### 3. Mask Application & Refinement
- The binary mask generated by GrabCut is applied to the original image using a bitwise AND operation (e.g., `cv2.bitwise_and()`), effectively blacking out the background.
- **Morphological Operations:** Techniques like Dilation and Erosion are applied to the mask to clean up rough edges, fill small holes inside the leaf area, and remove isolated noise pixels in the background.

## Usage

The notebook is designed for Google Colab. It guides you through loading the preprocessed images from the previous milestone, applying the GrabCut algorithm, and visualizing the original image alongside its segmented (background-removed) counterpart.
